Quantify Mobile — Shipments

Design collaboration doc for Brian, Blake, and Zino. Last updated April 2026.

What this doc is for: a shared reference so the whole team is aligned on what we're designing, who uses it, and why. Add your notes, questions, and feedback directly. Nothing here is final.

What We're Building

Yard workers at scaffolding companies spend their days loading and unloading trucks. Today, most of that counting happens on paper — a printed pick ticket with expected quantities. The worker checks off items, notes discrepancies by hand, and the office re-enters everything into Quantify later.

The mobile app replaces the paper step. Instead of a pick ticket, the worker picks up a tablet, opens the shipment, counts the equipment directly on screen, and confirms what actually went on (or came off) the truck. The office sees the real numbers instantly, no re-entry needed.

The core job
Count what goes on and off the truck. Confirm it's right. Flag discrepancies.
What changes
Paper pick tickets → digital count screen. No double entry. No lost paperwork.
Who benefits
Yard workers save 20–40 min per shipment. Office staff stop chasing paperwork.

Two main transaction types

Everything we're designing is about these two things:

DEL Delivery
Equipment leaves the yard and goes to a job site. Rent starts the moment it's confirmed. The yard worker counts what goes on the truck before it leaves.
RET Return
Equipment comes back from a job site to the yard. Rent stops when it's confirmed received. The yard worker counts what comes off the truck.

There are also Transfers (equipment moves between two locations, not to a job site) and Reservations (future-dated holds). We are not designing those in this phase.

The Users

Three types of people touch this flow. The yard worker is our primary focus for the mobile app.

Y
Yard Worker Primary user
Loads & unloads trucks at the yard
  • Works outdoors — sunlight glare, dusty hands, work gloves
  • Tablet is often shared with 2–3 other workers
  • Not a tech person — needs obvious, direct UI with no jargon
  • Counting quickly under pressure (truck is waiting)
  • May have limited English
  • Does not use desktop Quantify at all
  • Checks expected quantities, adjusts actuals, hits confirm
O
Office Staff
Creates shipments in desktop Quantify, handles disputes
  • Creates deliveries and pre-returns in desktop Quantify
  • Sets the expected quantities the yard worker will see
  • Sees confirmation results in real-time on desktop
  • Resolves discrepancies — decides if missing item is customer's fault or company's
  • Not a mobile user (desktop only in this phase)
C
Customer / Site Contact
Receives equipment at the job site (sometimes)
  • Only involved in Way 3 (customer confirmation workflow) — not in scope for phase 1
  • Counts what arrived at the job site and confirms independently
  • May access Quantify Web on their own device

Core Flows

Two workflows. Both follow the same pattern — open a shipment, count the items, confirm. The difference is direction.

Delivery Flow (Yard Worker)

Office creates a reservation in advance. Yard worker sees it on their tablet, counts what goes on the truck, confirms. Equipment is now "on rent."

1
Office creates reservation

Office staff creates a delivery in desktop Quantify: picks the job site, adds expected products and quantities, sets the planned date.

Office — desktop
2
Yard worker opens today's shipments

The worker sees a list of today's deliveries and returns. They tap the shipment they're loading now.

Yard worker — mobile
3
Count items

The count screen shows each product with its expected quantity. Worker taps + or − to enter the actual count. Items that differ from expected are highlighted.

Yard worker — mobile
4
Confirm shipment

Worker taps "Confirm." System releases the shipment — equipment is now on rent. Office sees the confirmed quantities immediately in desktop.

Yard worker — mobile
5
Discrepancy (if any)

If actual ≠ expected, office staff resolves it in desktop. They decide: customer's fault (customer pays) or company's fault (goes back to stock).

Office — desktop

Return Flow (Yard Worker)

Office creates a pre-return with expected quantities. Truck arrives at the yard. Worker counts what comes off the truck, confirms. Rent stops.

1
Office creates pre-return

Office sets the expected return date and expected quantities per product. A pick ticket can be printed and sent to the job site crew.

Office — desktop
2
Truck arrives at yard

Worker sees the pre-return in their list. Taps it.

Yard worker — mobile
3
Count items off the truck

Same count screen as delivery. Worker enters actual quantities as items come off. Equipment not on the original shipment can also be added — "over-returns" are allowed.

Yard worker — mobile
4
Flag condition (optional)

Worker can flag items as damaged, scrapped, or to-be-serviced. Normal items go straight to available stock. Damaged items go to the out-of-service queue.

Yard worker — mobile
5
Confirm return

Worker confirms. Rent stops immediately. Equipment enters the appropriate inventory state.

Yard worker — mobile

Edge case to design for: A worker might count more items than went out (e.g., a 10ft tube was cut into two 5ft tubes on site — both come back, only one went out). The system allows "negative balances." The count screen must let workers enter items beyond the expected quantity.

Design Rules

Rules the dev team must follow when building from these designs. These exist because mobile field workers have different needs than office software users.

👆
Minimum 56px touch targets
Every tappable element — buttons, list rows, +/− controls — must be at least 56px tall. Smaller targets cause mis-taps when wearing gloves or working in a hurry. Never reduce this for "cleaner" layouts.
☀️
High contrast text — no light grays
Primary text: #111111. Secondary text: #555555. Minimum. Workers read screens in direct sunlight. Never use grays lighter than #555 for readable text. Don't use #999, #AAA, or anything that looks "subtle" on a monitor — it's unreadable in a yard.
📐
Zero border-radius
All buttons, cards, inputs, and modals have border-radius: 0. This is a Quantify brand rule (Probe design language). No rounded corners, anywhere, ever. If you add rounded Tailwind classes they will be reverted.
🔤
Switzer font, 16px body minimum
Font family is Switzer (Google Fonts). Body text minimum 16px. Labels minimum 14px. Nothing smaller. On a tablet held at arm's length in sunlight, 14px is already pushing it.
🔵
Only one primary blue
Primary action color is #0A3EFF. Brand / nav color is #10296E (navy). Never use both on the same screen as interactive elements — this confused users in early prototypes. Use navy for headers/structure only.
📶
Design for offline-first
Many yard workers have poor cellular coverage inside warehouse structures. The app must work with a locally cached shipment list and sync when connection is available. Never design UI that assumes real-time connectivity. Always show a connection status indicator.
🌑
Light mode only for mobile
Do not implement dark mode for the mobile app. High contrast white background + dark text is significantly more readable in outdoor sunlight than dark mode. This was validated in feedback from the client.

Open Questions

Things we need Brian or Blake to answer before we can finalize the designs. These are not design questions — they're business logic questions.

1. Does the yard worker see ALL shipments for their branch, or only the ones assigned to them?
If a branch has 12 deliveries today and only 2 workers are on the tablet, should each worker see all 12 or only "their" shipments? Is there an assignment step, or do workers just grab whatever's next?
Ask: Brian Webb
2. Can a worker start a shipment that hasn't been created yet in desktop Quantify?
Sometimes trucks arrive unannounced. If the office hasn't created the return yet, does the yard worker wait, or can they start a count and the office confirms later? Is "ad hoc" counting in scope?
Ask: Brian Webb
3. When a worker flags an item as "damaged" — do they need to enter a reason or quantity?
In the current design, condition flagging is per-item (whole row = one condition). But what if 2 out of 18 ledger boards are damaged? Can the worker split one line? The current Quantify desktop limitation prevents splitting the same product into two conditions on the same return.
Ask: Brian Webb
4. Does the confirmation require a signature?
Desktop pick tickets have 1–3 signature lines (Prepared by / Delivered by / Received by). Does the mobile confirmation need a digital signature, or is tapping "Confirm" with a logged-in user enough for audit purposes?
Ask: Brian Webb, Blake Whittington
5. What does the worker see if they're offline?
Warehouses often have no signal. Should the app load shipment data when connected and allow counting offline, then sync when signal returns? Or is always-on connectivity a hard requirement? This changes the architecture significantly.
Ask: Brian Webb
6. How far ahead do reservations appear in the list?
If today is Monday and a delivery is scheduled for Wednesday, does it appear in Monday's list? Or do workers only see today's shipments? Does this vary by company or is it fixed?
Ask: Brian Webb
7. Is the app Uno Platform (Windows/mobile unified) or a separate React Native / web app?
This is a product/engineering decision that affects everything. Uno gives you one codebase for Windows + iOS + Android but adds complexity. A web app (this prototype) is faster to ship but has platform limitations. What's the final answer?
Ask: Blake Whittington, Brian Webb

Glossary

Scaffolding industry and Quantify terms. Non-designers: these are the words you'll hear in every meeting.

Term Meaning
DEL Delivery — equipment leaves the yard and goes to a job site. Rent starts.
RET Return — equipment comes back from the job site. Rent stops.
TRF Transfer — equipment moves between two yard/branch locations. Not to a job site.
RSV Reservation — future-dated hold. Removes stock from "available" but hasn't shipped yet.
Pre-Return A scheduled return with expected quantities set in advance. Lets the yard know what's coming off a truck before it arrives.
Pick Ticket Printed document showing what to load/unload. 1–3 signature lines. What the mobile app replaces.
On Rent Equipment is at a job site and billing is active.
In Transit Delivery was released by office but not yet confirmed received. Rent not yet started.
Discrepancy Difference between expected quantity (from shipment) and actual quantity (from counting).
Branch Office The main yard / stocking location. Where all equipment lives when not on a job site.
Job Site An active rental location. Equipment is "on rent" here. Has a customer, billing method, and rate profile.
Staging Area A free holding zone — equipment sits here without billing. Used at large industrial sites.
To Be Serviced Equipment returned but not ready for stock — needs inspection or repair first.
Rate Profile The pricing schedule assigned to a job site. Sets how much each product costs per rental period.
FATA First Advance Then Arrears. A billing method where the first invoice is pre-paid, then billing runs on arrears cycle.
Scaffold Tag Physical colored tag attached to a scaffold structure. Blue = not built, Red = cannot use, Green = safe, Yellow = use with caution.
